Conclusion:

The project achieved several key milestones, starting with the initiation of the Neos code and a thorough understanding of the discretization of operators and implementation of schemes. The Navier-Stokes equations for single-phase flow were solved using the prediction-correction method, marking a significant advancement in fluid dynamics research. The project then delved into the more complex scenario of two-phase flow, necessitating modifications in the approach throughout the resolution process. A comprehensive analysis was conducted on solving the Poisson equation using the CHP method, fully leveraging the parallel computing capabilities of the Bipit library. This integrated approach facilitated effective tackling of complex fluid dynamics problems.
