Temporally discretized version of Navier-Stokes equation can be written in the following form:

$$ \dfrac{u^{n+1}-u^{n}}{\Delta t}+ (u \cdot \nabla u)^{n+1} = -\nabla p^{n+1} + \dfrac{1}{Re}\Delta u^{n+1}, \quad \quad (1) $$

$$ \nabla u^{n+1}=0. \quad \quad (2) $$

Spatially discretized versions of the coupled equations (1) and (2) are difficult to solve directly. Therefore, a fractional step procedure can be used to approximate the solution of the coupled system by first solving an analog to equation (1) for an intermediate quantity u*, and then projecting this quantity onto the space of divergence-free fields to yield uⁿ⁺¹. In general, this procedure is given by:

Prediction Step:

The solution process begins with a prediction step. An intermediate velocity field, denoted u*, is estimated by solving a modified version of the momentum equation defined as follows:

$$ \dfrac{u^{*}-u^{n}}{\Delta t}+ (u \cdot \nabla u)^{*}= -\nabla p^{*} + \dfrac{1}{Re}\Delta u^{*}. $$

Poisson Equation:

Following the prediction step, the Poisson equation is employed to compute the pressure field. This equation serves to reconcile the intermediate velocity with the incompressibility condition by introducing a pressure correction term. We define ψ as a pressure correction function, where ψ = p* -pⁿ⁺¹. The Poisson equation can then be expressed in the following form:

$$ \dfrac{-\nabla u^{*}}{\Delta t}= \Delta (p^{*} -p^{n+1})= \Delta \psi. $$

Correction Step:

After updating the pression value pⁿ⁺¹= p* - ψ, the final step involves adjusting the intermediate velocity field with the pressure correction to obtain the velocity uⁿ⁺¹ at the next time step, thus ensuring both the momentum and continuity equations are satisfied:

$$ u^{n+1}=u^{*}+ \nabla \psi. $$

Through iterative application of these steps, the fluid's velocity and pressure fields are advanced in time, providing a numerical approximation to the Navier-Stokes equations.
